QUICK REFERENCE:
- MAASA = Lunar Month (approx. 29.5 days)
- PAKSHAM = Lunar Fortnight (approx. 15 days)
- Each Maasa has 2 Pakshams: Sukla (bright/waxing) and Krishna (dark/waning)
- Sukla Paksham: New Moon → Full Moon (good for new beginnings)
- Krishna Paksham: Full Moon → New Moon (good for reflection/ancestor worship)

================================================================================
                            SPECIAL NOTES
================================================================================

⭐ ADHIKA MAASA (Extra Month): May 18 - June 15, 2026
   - An intercalary month added every 2-3 years
   - Keeps lunar calendar aligned with solar year
   - Generally avoided for weddings and major new ventures

⭐ MAHALAYA PAKSHAM (Pitru Paksham): September 27 - October 10
   - Special 15-day period for honoring ancestors
   - Perform Tarpanam and Shraddha ceremonies
   - Ends with Mahalaya Amavasya (Oct 10)

⭐ IMPORTANT SOLAR TRANSITIONS:
   - Uttarayanam Begins: January 14, 2026 (Sun moves north)
   - Dakshinayanam Begins: July 17, 2026 (Sun moves south)
   - Dhanurmasam: December 16, 2026 - January 14, 2027

⭐ KARTIKA SOMAVARA (Auspicious Mondays):
   - All Mondays in Kartika Maasa (November 8 - December 7)
   - Special Siva Abhishekam performed


================================================================================
                              GLOSSARY
================================================================================

MAASA:        Lunar month (29.5 days)
PAKSHAM:      Lunar fortnight (15 days)
TITHI:        Lunar day
NAKSHATRA:    Lunar constellation/star
SUKLA:        Bright/waxing phase (moon growing)
KRISHNA:      Dark/waning phase (moon shrinking)
PURNIMA:      Full moon day (end of Sukla Paksham)
AMAVASYA:     New moon day (end of Krishna Paksham)
EKADASI:      11th day of Paksham (fasting day)
ADHIKA:       Extra/intercalary
NIJA:         Regular/original
MAHALAYA:     Period for ancestor worship
BRAHMOTSAVAM: Annual temple festival
UTTARAYANAM:  Sun's northward journey (auspicious)
DAKSHINAYANAM: Sun's southward journey
