SPECIAL NOVEMBER FESTIVALS:

NARAKA CHATURDASI (Nov 7):
Day before Deepavali. Celebrates Krishna's victory over demon Narakasura. Early morning oil bath before sunrise.

DEEPAVALI / DIWALI (Nov 8):
Festival of Lights. Most widely celebrated Hindu festival. Symbolizes victory of light over darkness, good over evil. Lighting of diyas (lamps), fireworks, exchange of sweets, Lakshmi puja, and family gatherings.

NAGA CHATURTHI / NAGULA CHAVITI (Nov 12):
Telugu festival for serpent worship. Women pray for well-being of brothers.

SRI MANAVALA MAHAMUNI TIRUNAKSHATRAM (Nov 12):
Birth star of Manavala Mamuni, important Sri Vaishnava acharya.

SRI VISHVAKSENA JAYANTI (Nov 14):
Birthday of Vishvaksena, commander-in-chief of Lord Vishnu's army. Worshiped as remover of obstacles in Vaishnavism.

SRI SKANDA SHASHTI (Nov 14):
Sixth day festival dedicated to Lord Murugan (Skanda). Celebrates his victory over demon Surapadman.

SRI TULASI KALYANAM (Nov 21):
Divine wedding of Tulasi (sacred basil plant) with Lord Vishnu/Shaligrama. Marks end of monsoon season and beginning of wedding season.

KARTIKA PURNIMA (Nov 23):
Full moon of Kartika month. Very auspicious for lighting lamps. Also called Tripuri Purnima.

KARTIKA DEEPAM (Nov 23):
Festival of lamps on Kartika Purnima. Especially significant in South India.

KARTIKA SOMAVARA (Throughout November):
Mondays in Kartika month are specially auspicious for Lord Shiva worship. Special Siva Abhishekams performed.
