SPECIAL JUNE FESTIVALS:

ANNUAL BRAHMOTSAVAM (Jun 17-20):
The most important and grandest festival of the temple. "Brahmotsavam" means "festival of Brahma" - considered so sacred that even Brahma would want to attend.

ANKURARPANAM (Jun 17):
Ceremonial planting of nine types of grains in earthen pots, marking the beginning of Brahmotsavam. The sprouting represents prosperity and divine blessings.

BRAHMOTSAVAM MAIN DAYS (Jun 18-19):
Multiple sevas and processions throughout the day including:
- Snapana Thirumanjanam (sacred bath)
- Various vahana sevas (deity mounted on different vehicles)
- Garuda Seva
- Hanumantha Vahanam
- Simha Vahanam
Each day has specific rituals and processions.

CHAKRA SNANAM / DWAJAVAROHANAM (Jun 20):
Final day ceremony where the temple flag is lowered. "Chakra Snanam" involves bathing the Sudarshana Chakra (divine discus). Marks the conclusion of Brahmotsavam with grand celebrations.

The Brahmotsavam is a time of intense devotion, special poojas, cultural programs, and large gatherings. Devotees from across the region attend. Special sevas can be sponsored.

NIJA JYESHTA MAASA:
Starting June 14, the regular (Nija) Jyeshta month begins after the Adhika (leap) month ends.
