SPECIAL OCTOBER FESTIVALS:

MAHALAYA AMAVASYA (Oct 10):
Most important day of Pitru Paksha. Final day for performing Shraddha (ancestral rites). Marks beginning of Navaratri. Tarpana offerings made to ancestors.

SARANNAVARATRI / NAVARATRI (Oct 11-20):
Nine-night festival worshiping Divine Mother in her various forms:
- Days 1-3: Goddess Durga (destroyer of evil)
- Days 4-6: Goddess Lakshmi (bestower of wealth)
- Days 7-9: Goddess Saraswati (giver of knowledge)

SARASWATI PUJA (Oct 16):
Seventh day of Navaratri dedicated to Goddess Saraswati. Books and musical instruments are worshiped. Auspicious for starting education.

DURGASHTAMI (Oct 18):
Eighth day of Navaratri. Most important day for Durga worship. Special poojas and kumari (young girl) worship.

MAHARNAVAMI / AYUDHA PUJA (Oct 19):
Ninth day - worship of weapons, tools, and vehicles. In South India, all implements of livelihood are worshiped.

VIJAYA DASAMI (Oct 20):
Victory of Goddess Durga over demon Mahishasura. Also celebrates Lord Rama's victory over Ravana. Most auspicious day to begin new ventures, education, and learning.
