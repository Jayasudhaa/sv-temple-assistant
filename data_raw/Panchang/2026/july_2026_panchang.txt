SPECIAL JULY FESTIVALS:

SANI TRAYODASI (Jul 11):
Trayodasi falling on Saturday. Auspicious for Lord Shani (Saturn) worship.

DAKSHINAYANAM BEGINS (Jul 17):
Marks the sun's southward journey. From this day until January, the sun travels south of the equator. This six-month period is considered auspicious for spiritual practices, though not for new ventures. Opposite of Uttarayanam.

TOLI EKADASI (Jul 24):
"Toli" means first in Telugu. First Ekadasi of Dakshinayanam, hence highly significant. Also called Shayani Ekadasi - Lord Vishnu goes into yogic sleep (Yoga Nidra) for four months (Chaturmasya).

GURU PURNIMA (Jul 28):
Festival honoring spiritual and academic teachers (Gurus). On this day, disciples express gratitude to their teachers. Associated with Sage Vyasa, who composed the Mahabharata and Puranas. Also called Vyasa Purnima.

CHATURMASYA PERIOD BEGINS:
Starting from Shayani Ekadasi (Jul 24), the four-month period of Chaturmasya begins. This is a time for:
- Increased spiritual practices
- Fasting on specific days
- Avoiding certain foods (varies by tradition)
- Extra prayers and devotional activities
