SPECIAL APRIL FESTIVALS:

TAMIL NEW YEAR / VISHU (Apr 14):
Tamil and Malayalam New Year celebration. Vishu is celebrated in Kerala with special arrangements of auspicious items (Vishukkani) viewed first thing in the morning. Tamil New Year involves special prayers, new clothes, and festive meals.

AKSHAYA TRITIYA (Apr 19):
One of the most auspicious days in Hindu calendar. "Akshaya" means imperishable/eternal. Considered highly favorable for:
- Starting new ventures
- Buying gold and property
- Weddings and important ceremonies
- Charity and donations
Any activity begun on this day is believed to bring lasting prosperity. Associated with birth of Parasurama avatar and beginning of Treta Yuga.

SRI SANKARACHARYA JAYANTI (Apr 21):
Birthday of Adi Shankaracharya, the great philosopher and theologian who established Advaita Vedanta philosophy and organized Hindu monasticism. He lived in 8th century CE and established four major mathas (monasteries) across India.

SRI RAMANUJA JAYANTI (Apr 22):
Birthday of Sri Ramanujacharya (1017-1137 CE), the great Vaishnava philosopher and proponent of Vishishtadvaita (qualified non-dualism). He organized temple worship and wrote commentaries on Brahma Sutras.

SRI NRUSIMHA JAYANTI (Apr 30):
Birthday of Lord Narasimha, the fourth avatar of Vishnu - half-man, half-lion. Celebrated with special poojas and abhishekams. Narasimha appeared to protect devotee Prahlada and destroy demon Hiranyakashipu.
