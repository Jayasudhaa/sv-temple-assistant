NOVEMBER 27TH, 2025 - THANKSGIVING DAY




SIGNIFICANCE:
Tulasi Kalyanam (also called Tulasi Vivah) celebrates the sacred marriage of the holy Tulasi plant (Holy Basil) to Lord Vishnu in his Shaligram form. This marks the beginning of the Hindu wedding season and the end of the four-month Chaturmas period.

MYTHOLOGY:
According to Hindu scriptures, Tulasi Devi was a devoted woman named Vrinda who was transformed into the sacred Tulasi plant. Her marriage to Lord Vishnu is celebrated annually on this auspicious day.

TEMPLE CELEBRATIONS:
- Traditional wedding ceremony performed for Tulasi plant
- Tulasi Devi adorned as a bride
- Shaligram (form of Vishnu) as the groom
- Vedic mantras and traditional marriage rituals
- Circumambulation (pradakshina) around Tulasi
- Special prasadam distribution

DEVOTIONAL PRACTICE:
- Devotees can bring flowers, fruits for offering
- Participate in the marriage ceremony
- Seek blessings for their own marriages
- Water Tulasi plant as part of daily worship

SPIRITUAL BENEFITS:
- Blessings for married life
- Removal of obstacles in finding suitable match
- Spiritual purification
- Starting auspicious activities after Chaturmas

WHAT TO BRING:
- Flowers for decoration
- Fruits for offering
- Traditional wedding items (optional)
