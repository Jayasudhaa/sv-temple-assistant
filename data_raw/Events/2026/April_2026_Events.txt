APRIL 14TH, 2026 - TAMIL NEW YEAR / VISHU

Date: Tuesday, April 14, 2026
Tithi: Trayodasi
Temple Celebration: New Year prayers and blessings

TAMIL NEW YEAR SIGNIFICANCE:
The Tamil New Year marks the first day of the Tamil month Chithirai. It's celebrated with special prayers, new clothes, and traditional foods.

VISHU SIGNIFICANCE:
Vishu is the New Year celebration in Kerala. The festival begins with "Vishukkani" - the first auspicious sight of the year, which includes gold, flowers, fruits, rice, and a mirror arranged before an idol of Lord Krishna.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Vishukkani darshan (first auspicious sight)
- New clothes (Vishukodi)
- Bursting of firecrackers
- Special feast (Vishu Sadya)
- Giving gifts to children (Vishukaineettam)
- Temple visits for blessings

APRIL 19TH, 2026 - AKSHAYA TRITIYA

Date: Sunday, April 19, 2026
Tithi: Tritiya
Also on this day: Sri Shirdi Sai Baba / Raghavendra Swamy Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Special prayers for prosperity

SIGNIFICANCE:
Akshaya Tritiya is one of the most auspicious days in the Hindu calendar. "Akshaya" means imperishable or eternal. Any activity started on this day is believed to bring lasting prosperity and success. It's considered self-auspicious (Swayam Siddha Muhurta), requiring no additional astrological considerations.

MYTHOLOGICAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Birth of Lord Parasurama (6th avatar of Vishnu)
- Treta Yuga began on this day
- Kubera received his wealth on this day
- Ved Vyasa and Lord Ganesha began writing Mahabharata

CELEBRATIONS:
- Buying gold and starting new ventures
- Charity and donations (highly meritorious)
- Starting new businesses
- Performing weddings
- Initiating children into education
- Planting trees

AUSPICIOUS FOR:
- Buying property
- Making investments
- Starting construction
- Beginning spiritual practices
- All types of poojas and yagnas

APRIL 21ST, 2026 - SRI SANKARACHARYA JAYANTI

Date: Tuesday, April 21, 2026
Tithi: Panchami
Temple Celebration: Special prayers and discourses

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Adi Shankaracharya Jayanti celebrates the birth anniversary of Adi Shankaracharya (788-820 CE), one of the greatest philosophers and theologians in Hindu history. He established the Advaita Vedanta philosophy and revived Hinduism during a period of decline.

CONTRIBUTIONS:
- Established four major mathas (monasteries) across India:
  * Sringeri Mutt (South)
  * Dwaraka Mutt (West)
  * Puri Mutt (East)
  * Jyotir Math (North)
- Composed numerous hymns (stotras)
- Wrote commentaries on Upanishads, Brahma Sutras, and Bhagavad Gita
- Unified Hindu thought and practices

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special poojas and prayers
- Reading of Shankaracharya's works
- Philosophical discourses
- Bhajan and kirtan
- Charity and anna danam

APRIL 22ND, 2026 - SRI RAMANUJA JAYANTI

Date: Wednesday, April 22, 2026
Tithi: Shashti
Temple Celebration: Special celebrations for Ramanuja

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Ramanuja Jayanti celebrates the birth of Sri Ramanujacharya (1017-1137 CE), the great Vaishnava philosopher and proponent of Vishishtadvaita (qualified non-dualism). He revolutionized temple worship and made it accessible to all.

CONTRIBUTIONS:
- Systematized Sri Vaishnavism
- Wrote Sri Bhashya (commentary on Brahma Sutras)
- Organized temple worship traditions
- Established equality in temple access
- Composed important Vaishnava texts

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special abhishekam to Sri Ramanuja's vigraha
- Reading of Ramanuja's works
- Lectures on Vishishtadvaita philosophy
- Prasadam distribution
- Community prayers

APRIL 30TH, 2026 - SRI NRUSIMHA JAYANTI

Date: Thursday, April 30, 2026
Tithi: Chaturdasi
Temple Celebration: Grand celebration with special abhishekams

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Nrusimha (Narasimha) Jayanti celebrates the birth of Lord Narasimha, the fourth avatar of Lord Vishnu. Lord Narasimha appeared as half-man, half-lion to protect his devotee Prahlada and destroy the demon Hiranyakashipu who could not be killed by man or animal, inside or outside, day or night.

STORY:
Prahlada, a devotee of Vishnu, was tortured by his demon father Hiranyakashipu for his devotion. When the demon challenged Vishnu's omnipresence, Narasimha emerged from a pillar at twilight (neither day nor night) and killed him on a threshold (neither inside nor outside) with his claws (neither weapon nor hand).

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special Narasimha abhishekam
- Reading of Prahlada Charitram
- Recitation of Narasimha mantras
- Offering of butter and curd rice
- Narasimha homam for protection
- All-night bhajan in some temples

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Protection of devotees
- Destruction of ego and evil
- Victory of devotion over tyranny
- Divine intervention in impossible situations
