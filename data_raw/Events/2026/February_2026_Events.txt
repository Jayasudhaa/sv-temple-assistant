FEBRUARY 1, 2026 - TAIPOOSAM

Date: Sunday, February 1, 2026
Tithi: Purnima (Full Moon)
Nakshatra: Pushyami
Also on this day: Sri Siva Abhishekam

SIGNIFICANCE:
Taipoosam (or Thaipusam) is a Tamil festival dedicated to Lord Murugan (Subramanya), the son of Lord Shiva. It celebrates the victory of good over evil and commemorates the day when Goddess Parvati gave the divine spear (Vel) to Lord Murugan to defeat the demon Surapadman. The festival occurs when the Pushya Nakshatra coincides with the full moon in the Tamil month of Thai.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special abhishekam to Lord Murugan
- Kavadi (burden) carrying by devotees
- Fasting and prayers
- Processions with Lord Murugan's idol
- Recitation of Murugan songs and hymns
- Milk abhishekam

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Represents triumph of light over darkness
- Devotees undertake penances and vows
- Seeking blessings for strength and courage

FEBRUARY 14, 2026 - SANI TRAYODASI

Date: Saturday, February 14, 2026
Tithi: Trayodasi
Also on this day: Sri Venkateswara Kalyanam

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sani Trayodasi occurs when Trayodasi (13th lunar day) falls on Saturday (Sani-vara). This is considered highly auspicious for prayers to Lord Shani (Saturn deity). Worshiping on this day is believed to alleviate Saturn-related difficulties and bring relief from obstacles.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special Shani pooja
- Offering of black sesame seeds and oil
- Lighting oil lamps
- Charity to the needy
- Recitation of Shani mantras

OBSERVANCES:
Devotees visit Shani temples, offer prayers, and perform rituals to appease Lord Shani.

FEBRUARY 15, 2026 - MAHA SIVARATRI

Date: Sunday/Monday, February 15-16, 2026
Tithi: Amavasya (New Moon)
Nakshatra: Sravana
Also on this day: Sri Shirdi Sai Baba / Raghavendra Swamy Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: All-night vigil with special poojas

SIGNIFICANCE:
Maha Sivaratri, "The Great Night of Shiva," is the most important festival dedicated to Lord Shiva. It marks the night when Lord Shiva performed the Tandava (cosmic dance of creation, preservation, and destruction). It is also celebrated as the night of Shiva's wedding to Goddess Parvati. This is the most sacred night for Shiva devotees.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Night-long vigil (jagaran)
- Four special abhishekams during the night (prahar poojas)
- Fasting (complete or with fruits/milk)
- Chanting "Om Namah Shivaya"
- Offering of bel leaves, milk, honey, water
- Rudra abhishekam
- Bilva archana (worship with bel leaves)

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Overcoming darkness and ignorance
- Union of Shiva and Shakti
- Path to moksha (liberation)
- Cleansing of sins
- Awakening of spiritual consciousness

OBSERVANCE:
Devotees stay awake all night, performing pooja at four different prahar (time periods). Many observe complete fast and break it only the next morning after sunrise.

SPECIAL RITUALS:
1. First Prahar (Evening): Abhishekam with milk
2. Second Prahar (Late night): Abhishekam with yogurt/curd
3. Third Prahar (Midnight): Abhishekam with ghee
4. Fourth Prahar (Pre-dawn): Abhishekam with honey
