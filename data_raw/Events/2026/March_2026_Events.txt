MARCH 2, 2026 - HOLI / LUNAR ECLIPSE / SRI MAHALAKSHMI JAYANTI

Date: Monday, March 2, 2026
Tithi: Purnima (Full Moon)
Nakshatra: Magha
Also on this day: Sri Satyanarayana Swamy Pooja & Vratham
Temple Celebration: Special observances with eclipse rituals

HOLI SIGNIFICANCE:
Holi, the "Festival of Colors," celebrates the victory of good over evil, commemorating the story of Prahlada and Holika. It marks the arrival of spring and the end of winter. Holi symbolizes the triumph of devotion over evil.

HOLI CELEBRATIONS:
- Playing with colors (gulal)
- Holika Dahan (bonfire) on the evening before
- Singing and dancing
- Traditional sweets (gujiya, malpua)
- Community gatherings
- Forgiveness and renewal of relationships

LUNAR ECLIPSE:
A lunar eclipse occurs on this Purnima. Special prayers and rituals are performed during eclipse time. Devotees take bath after eclipse and perform pooja.

SRI MAHALAKSHMI JAYANTI:
Birthday of Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of wealth, prosperity, and fortune. Special prayers for prosperity and well-being.

MARCH 19, 2026 - SRI PARABHAVA NAAMA SAMVATSARA UGADI

Date: Thursday, March 19, 2026
Tithi: Dwitiya
Also Known As: Yugadi, Telugu/Kannada New Year
Also on this day: Sri Andal Abhishekam, Sri Varaha Jayanti
Temple Celebration: Grand New Year celebration

SIGNIFICANCE:
Ugadi marks the New Year for people in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka. "Ugadi" comes from "Yuga" (age) and "Adi" (beginning), meaning "the beginning of a new age." This year is "Parabhava Naama Samvatsara." Ugadi is the official beginning of the Hindu lunar calendar year.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Ugadi Pachadi preparation (six tastes representing life's experiences)
- Panchan Shravanam (reading of the year's predictions)
- House cleaning and decorations
- Mango leaf torans on doorways
- New clothes
- Special prayers and poojas
- Family gatherings and feast

UGADI PACHADI - Six Tastes:
1. Sweet (jaggery) - Happiness
2. Sour (tamarind) - Challenges
3. Bitter (neem) - Sadness
4. Salty (salt) - Interests/Desires
5. Spicy (chili) - Anger/Passion
6. Astringent (raw mango) - Surprise

SRI VARAHA JAYANTI:
Also celebrated on Ugadi - birthday of Lord Varaha (boar incarnation of Vishnu who rescued Earth from the cosmic ocean).

MARCH 26, 2026 - SRI RAMA NAVAMI

Date: Thursday, March 26, 2026
Tithi: Navami
Nakshatra: Punarvasu
Temple Celebration: Grand celebration with special rituals

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Rama Navami celebrates the birthday of Lord Rama, the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu and the hero of the epic Ramayana. Lord Rama was born in Ayodhya to King Dasharatha and Queen Kausalya on the ninth day (Navami) of Chaitra month during bright fortnight (Sukla Paksha).

CELEBRATIONS:
- Reading of Ramayana (Rama Katha)
- Rama Pattabhishekam (coronation ceremony)
- Rama Kalyanam (divine wedding - on following days)
- Nine-day celebration (Rama Navami falls on 9th day)
- Special abhishekam and alankaram to Sri Rama
- Bhajan and kirtan
- Processions with Rama's idol
- Distribution of panakam (jaggery drink) and prasadam

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Embodiment of dharma (righteousness)
- Victory of good over evil
- Ideal king, son, brother, and husband
- Following the path of truth and duty

OBSERVANCES:
- Fasting (complete or partial)
- Recitation of Rama Nama
- Reading Sundara Kanda from Ramayana
- Charity and feeding the poor

MARCH 28, 2026 - SRI SITARAMA KALYANAM

Date: Saturday, March 28, 2026
Tithi: Dwadasi
Also on this day: Sri Hanuman Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Divine wedding ceremony

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Sitarama Kalyanam celebrates the divine wedding of Lord Rama and Goddess Sita. The marriage is re-enacted with great devotion and traditional rituals, symbolizing the perfect union and ideal relationship.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Wedding ceremony re-enactment
- Special decorations and mandapam
- Traditional wedding rituals
- Offering of sarees and ornaments
- Special alankaram to deities
- Distribution of wedding prasadam
- Cultural programs depicting Ramayana scenes

SPIRITUAL MESSAGE:
The marriage of Rama and Sita represents the union of dharma (righteousness) and devotion, serving as an ideal for married life.

MARCH 31, 2026 - PANGUNI UTTARAM

Date: Tuesday, March 31, 2026
Tithi: Purnima (approaching)
Nakshatra: Uttaraphalguni
Temple Celebration: Special deity weddings and celebrations

SIGNIFICANCE:
Panguni Uttaram is a significant Tamil festival associated with the Uttaraphalguni nakshatra in the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April). It celebrates divine weddings, particularly:
- Marriage of Goddess Parvati to Lord Shiva
- Marriage of Lord Murugan to Goddess Deivayanai
- Various other divine kalyanams

CELEBRATIONS:
- Temple processions with decorated deities
- Re-enactment of divine marriages
- Special alankaram and decorations
- Offering of garlands and flowers
- Cultural performances
- Community feasts

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
Panguni Uttaram is considered highly auspicious for weddings and new beginnings. The day signifies divine blessings for marital harmony and prosperity.
