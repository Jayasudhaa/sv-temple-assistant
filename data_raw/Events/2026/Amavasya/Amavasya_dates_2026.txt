SPECIAL AMAVASYA OBSERVANCES:

Maha Sivaratri (February 16):
- Most important festival dedicated to Lord Shiva
- Night-long vigil and fasting observed
- Special poojas and abhishekams performed

Mahalaya Amavasya (October 10):
- Marks the end of Pitru Paksham (fortnight for ancestral offerings)
- Highly auspicious for Tarpana (offerings to ancestors)
- Beginning of Navaratri/Durga Puja season

Deepavali/Diwali (November 8):
- Festival of Lights
- Most celebrated Hindu festival
- Preceded by Naraka Chaturdasi on November 7

Sri Sanischara Jayanti (May 16):
- Birth anniversary of Lord Shani (Saturn deity)
- Special prayers for Shani Dev

PITRU PAKSHAM 2026:
The fifteen-day period for ancestral rites begins after the Purnima in September and ends on Mahalaya Amavasya (October 10, 2026). During this period, Hindus pay homage to their ancestors through Shraddha ceremonies.
