NOVEMBER 7TH, 2026 - NARAKA CHATURDASI

Date: Saturday, November 7, 2026
Tithi: Chaturdasi (one day before Deepavali)
Also on this day: Sri Venkateswara Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Pre-Deepavali observances

SIGNIFICANCE:
Naraka Chaturdasi, also called Choti Diwali or Roop Chaturdashi, is celebrated one day before Deepavali. It commemorates Lord Krishna's victory over the demon Narakasura, who had imprisoned 16,100 daughters of gods and saints.

LEGEND:
Narakasura, son of Mother Earth, became arrogant with power and terrorized the three worlds. Lord Krishna, with his consort Satyabhama, killed Narakasura and freed the imprisoned princesses. Before dying, Narakasura requested that his death be celebrated, hence this festival.

OBSERVANCES:
- Abhyanga Snanam (oil bath) before sunrise
- Applying ubtan (herbal paste)
- Lighting oil lamps in the evening
- Bursting small firecrackers
- Preparing special sweets
- Cleaning and decorating homes

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Victory of light over darkness
- Destruction of ego and evil
- Purification before Deepavali

NOVEMBER 8TH, 2026 - DEEPAVALI (DIWALI)

Date: Sunday, November 8, 2026
Tithi: Amavasya (New Moon)
Also Known As: Diwali, Festival of Lights
Also on this day: Sri Vijaya Ganapathi / Murugan Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Grand celebration with lakhs of lights

SIGNIFICANCE:
Deepavali, the "Festival of Lights," is the most widely celebrated Hindu festival. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. The festival is celebrated across India and by Hindus worldwide with different regional stories and customs.

PRIMARY LEGENDS:
1. North India: Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile and defeating Ravana
2. South India: Lord Krishna's victory over demon Narakasura
3. West India: Lord Vishnu sending demon king Bali to netherworld
4. Jainism: Lord Mahavira attaining nirvana
5. Sikhism: Release of Guru Hargobind Ji from imprisonment

GODDESS LAKSHMI:
Deepavali is especially dedicated to Goddess Lakshmi, deity of wealth and prosperity. This is the main Lakshmi Puja day of the year.

PREPARATIONS:
- Thorough cleaning of homes and workplaces
- Decorating with rangoli/kolam
- Whitewashing and painting
- Shopping for new clothes
- Buying gold and silver
- Preparing special sweets

CELEBRATIONS:
- Lakshmi Puja in evening (most auspicious time)
- Lighting diyas (oil lamps) throughout home
- Decorating with electric lights
- Bursting firecrackers (in moderation)
- Exchanging gifts and sweets
- Wearing new clothes
- Family gatherings and feast
- Playing cards (traditional)
- Business community: closing old account books, opening new ones

SPIRITUAL OBSERVANCES:
- Invoke Goddess Lakshmi for prosperity
- Light lamps to dispel darkness/ignorance
- Offer prayers for family well-being
- Charity to the less fortunate
- Feeding the poor

FIVE DAYS OF DIWALI (Extended Celebration):
Though Deepavali is primarily one day, traditionally it's celebrated over five days:
- Day 1: Dhanteras (2 days before)
- Day 2: Naraka Chaturdasi/Choti Diwali (1 day before)
- Day 3: Deepavali/Lakshmi Puja (Main day - Nov 8)
- Day 4: Govardhan Puja/Annakut (1 day after)
- Day 5: Bhai Dooj (2 days after)

IMPORTANT RITUALS:
- Cleaning and purifying the home
- Creating rangoli at entrance
- Lighting 108 or 365 diyas
- Offering sweets to Lakshmi
- Keeping doors/windows open for Lakshmi's entry
- New account books for businesses

ECO-FRIENDLY CELEBRATION:
- Using clay diyas instead of electric lights when possible
- Avoiding loud and polluting firecrackers
- Choosing green crackers
- Celebrating responsibly

NOVEMBER 12TH, 2026 - NAGA CHATURTHI (NAGULA CHAVITI)

Date: Thursday, November 12, 2026
Tithi: Chaturthi
Also on this day: Sri Manavala Mahamuni Tirunakshatram
Temple Celebration: Serpent worship observances

SIGNIFICANCE:
Naga Chaturthi, also called Nagula Chaviti in Telugu, is a festival dedicated to serpent worship. It's particularly important in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, where women fast and pray for the well-being of their brothers and male family members.

LEGEND:
Based on the story of a woman whose brothers were killed by snake bite. Through devotion and prayers, she brought them back to life.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Women observe fasting
- Worship of snake idols or images
- Drawing snake designs with turmeric and kumkum
- Offering milk and eggs (traditional)
- Prayers for protection from snake bites
- Sisters praying for brothers' welfare

SRI MANAVALA MAHAMUNI TIRUNAKSHATRAM:
Birth star of Manavala Mamuni, important Sri Vaishnava acharya of the 15th century.

NOVEMBER 14TH, 2026 - SRI VISHVAKSENA JAYANTI / SRI SKANDA SHASHTI

Date: Saturday, November 14, 2026
Tithi: Panchami/Shashti
Also on this day: Sri Venkateswara Kalyanam
Temple Celebration: Dual celebration

SRI VISHVAKSENA JAYANTI SIGNIFICANCE:
Birthday of Vishvaksena, the commander-in-chief of Lord Vishnu's army. In Sri Vaishnavism, Vishvaksena is worshiped as the remover of obstacles before beginning any pooja or ritual.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special Vishvaksena pooja
- Prayers before main temple rituals
- Seeking blessings for obstacle-free worship

SRI SKANDA SHASHTI SIGNIFICANCE:
Six-day festival culminating on Shashti (6th day), dedicated to Lord Murugan (Skanda). Celebrates Murugan's victory over the demon Surapadman.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Kavadi (burden) carrying by devotees
- Fasting for six days
- Reading Skanda Purana
- Vel (spear) worship
- Temple processions

NOVEMBER 21ST, 2026 - SRI TULASI KALYANAM

Date: Saturday, November 21, 2026
Tithi: Trayodasi/Dwadasi
Also on this day: Sri Mahalakshmi Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Divine wedding ceremony

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Tulasi Kalyanam celebrates the ceremonial wedding of the sacred Tulasi plant (holy basil) to Lord Vishnu or his Shaligrama form. This festival marks the end of the monsoon season and the beginning of the Hindu wedding season.

LEGEND:
Based on the story of Vrinda (Tulasi), a devoted wife whose virtue protected her demon husband Jalandhar. After deception led to his death, she became the Tulasi plant and was wed to Lord Vishnu.

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Tulasi is considered the most sacred plant in Hinduism
- Represents devotion and purity
- Marriage to Vishnu symbolizes union of devotee with divine
- After this day, Hindu weddings can be performed (before Dev Uthani Ekadasi)

CELEBRATIONS:
- Installing Tulasi Vrindavan (sacred enclosure)
- Decorating Tulasi plant as bride
- Shaligrama stone as groom
- Traditional wedding rituals
- Circumambulation of Tulasi
- Offering of new clothes and jewelry
- Lighting lamps around Tulasi
- Distribution of prasadam

OBSERVANCES:
Women who observe fast for husband's longevity participate actively. This is also called Tulasi Vivah.

NOVEMBER 23RD, 2026 - KARTIKA PURNIMA / KARTIKA DEEPAM

Date: Monday, November 23, 2026
Tithi: Purnima (Full Moon)
Also on this day: Kartika Somavara Siva Abhishekam, Sri Satyanarayana Pooja
Temple Celebration: Festival of lights for Lord Shiva

SIGNIFICANCE:
Kartika Purnima is the full moon day of Kartika month, one of the most sacred days in Hindu calendar. Also called Tripuri Purnima, it celebrates multiple divine events.

KARTIKA DEEPAM SIGNIFICANCE:
Festival of lighting lamps, especially significant in South India where it celebrates Lord Shiva appearing as an endless pillar of light (Jyoti). At Tiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu, a huge lamp is lit atop Arunachala Hill.

MYTHOLOGICAL EVENTS ON THIS DAY:
- Lord Shiva destroyed three demon cities (Tripura)
- Birth of Kartikeya (Murugan)
- Matsya Avatar of Vishnu appeared
- Lord Vishnu's Prabodhini Ekadasi (waking from yoga nidra - occurs day before)

CELEBRATIONS:
- Lighting lamps throughout home and temples
- Special Siva pooja
- Kartika Snanam (holy bath)
- Charity and donations (highly meritorious)
- Offering of lamps in temples
- Feeding the poor
- Pilgrimage to sacred rivers

KARTIKA MAASA IMPORTANCE:
The entire month of Kartika (October-November) is sacred. Throughout this month:
- Daily lamp offerings
- Fasting on Ekadasis
- Special Somavara (Monday) Siva worship
- Reading Bhagavad Gita or Bhagavatam
- Extra spiritual practices
