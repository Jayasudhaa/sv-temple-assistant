AUGUST 6, 2026 - ADI KRITTIKA

Date: Thursday, August 6, 2026
Tithi: Dasami
Nakshatra: Krittika
Temple Celebration: Special Murugan pooja

SIGNIFICANCE:
Adi Krittika is a Tamil festival dedicated to Lord Murugan (Karthikeya), celebrated when Krittika nakshatra falls in the Tamil month of Adi (mid-July to mid-August). Krittika is the birth star of Lord Murugan.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special abhishekam to Lord Murugan
- Offering of kavadi (burden carrying)
- Temple processions
- Singing of Tamil devotional songs
- Distribution of prasadam

AUGUST 11, 2026 - SRI RAGHAVENDRA SWAMY ARADHANA

Date: Tuesday, August 11, 2026
Tithi: Amavasya (New Moon)
Temple Celebration: Special commemoration

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Raghavendra Swamy Aradhana commemorates the day when Sri Raghavendra Swamy entered Brindavan (living samadhi) in 1671 at Mantralayam. This is one of the most important festivals for followers of the Madhwa (Dvaita) tradition.

SRI RAGHAVENDRA SWAMY:
- Great saint and philosopher of 17th century
- Composed numerous works on Dvaita philosophy
- Known for miraculous powers and compassion
- Established at Mantralayam, Andhra Pradesh
- Believed to be in perpetual meditation in Brindavan

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special abhishekam to Raghavendra Swamy
- Reading of his works and life story
- Offering of sweet payasam (his favorite)
- All-day bhajan and kirtan
- Distribution of prasadam
- Charity and feeding the poor

AUGUST 14, 2026 - SRI ANDAL JAYANTI

Date: Friday, August 14, 2026
Tithi: Dwitiya/Tritiya
Nakshatra: Purvaphalguni
Also on this day: Sri Andal Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Grand birthday celebration

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Andal Jayanti celebrates the birth of Sri Andal (Goda Devi), the only female among the twelve Alvars (Vaishnava saint poets). She was found as a baby under a Tulasi plant by Periyalvar in Srivilliputhur, Tamil Nadu. Andal is considered an incarnation of Bhudevi (Mother Earth).

ANDAL'S CONTRIBUTIONS:
- Composed Thiruppavai (30 verses)
- Composed Nachiyar Thirumozhi (143 verses)
- Exemplifies pure devotion (Madhurya Bhakti)
- Represents devotee's longing for union with divine
- Her works are recited daily in Vaishnava temples

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special alankaram (decoration) to Andal
- Recitation of Thiruppavai
- Goda Kalyanam (divine wedding reenactment)
- Flower decorations
- Cultural programs depicting Andal's life
- Distribution of prasadam

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
Andal's life teaches complete surrender to God and the path of devotional love (Bhakti).

AUGUST 16, 2026 - GARUDA PANCHAMI / NAGA PANCHAMI

Date: Sunday, August 16, 2026
Tithi: Panchami/Shashti
Also on this day: Sri Shirdi Sai Baba Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Dual celebration

GARUDA PANCHAMI SIGNIFICANCE:
Honors Lord Garuda, the divine vehicle (vahana) of Lord Vishnu. Garuda, the king of birds, symbolizes power, devotion, and the ability to overcome obstacles.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Special Garuda pooja
- Recitation of Garuda Purana
- Prayers for protection from snake bites
- Offering of special naivedyam

NAGA PANCHAMI SIGNIFICANCE:
Festival dedicated to serpent deities (Nagas). Snakes are worshiped as symbols of fertility, protection, and cosmic energy. In Hindu mythology, snakes have divine significance.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Worship of snake idols or pictures
- Offering milk to snakes (traditional)
- Prayers for protection from snake bites
- Women tie sacred threads for brothers' protection
- Drawing snake images with turmeric and kumkum

AUGUST 21, 2026 - SRI VARALAKSHMI VRATAM

Date: Friday, August 21, 2026
Tithi: Ekadasi
Temple Celebration: Special Lakshmi pooja for women

SIGNIFICANCE:
Sri Varalakshmi Vratam is one of the most important festivals for married Hindu women, primarily observed in South India. Women pray to Goddess Lakshmi (consort of Lord Vishnu) for the well-being, prosperity, and long life of their husbands and families.

LEGEND:
Based on a story where Goddess Parvati advised a devoted woman named Charumathi to worship Goddess Varalakshmi on this day, promising fulfillment of wishes.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Women observe fast (complete or partial)
- Special kalasam (sacred pot) installation
- Worshiping Varalakshmi (boon-granting Lakshmi)
- Tying of sacred thread (kappu/raksha)
- Offering traditional naivedyam
- Inviting married women (Sumangalis)
- Exchanging gifts and betel leaves
- Seeking blessings for family prosperity

PREPARATIONS:
- Decorating pooja room elaborately
- Creating special rangoli/kolam
- Preparing traditional sweets and savories
- Dressing in traditional attire and jewelry

AUGUST 26, 2026 - RIGVEDA UPAKARMA

Date: Wednesday, August 26, 2026
Tithi: Prathama
Temple Celebration: Sacred thread ceremony for Rigvedis

SIGNIFICANCE:
Upakarma is the annual ritual of changing the sacred thread (Yajnopavita) and beginning new study of Vedas. Rigveda Upakarma is observed by Brahmins following the Rigveda tradition.

RITUALS:
- Holy bath in sacred waters or rivers
- Removal of old sacred thread
- Wearing new sacred thread with Vedic chants
- Performing rituals with family priest
- Beginning of Vedic study for the year

AUGUST 27, 2026 - YAJURVEDA UPAKARMA / AVANI AVITTAM / RAKHI PURNIMA / LUNAR ECLIPSE

Date: Thursday, August 27, 2026
Tithi: Dwitiya (after Purnima)
Also Known As: Raksha Bandhan
Also on this day: Sri Satyanarayana Pooja & Vratham
Temple Celebration: Multiple auspicious observances with eclipse rituals

YAJURVEDA UPAKARMA / AVANI AVITTAM SIGNIFICANCE:
Sacred thread changing ceremony for Yajurvedi Brahmins - the most widely observed Upakarma. "Avani Avittam" is the Tamil name for this festival.

RITUALS:
- Holy bath before sunrise
- Wearing new sacred thread
- Kamokarsheet mantra (seeking forgiveness for sins)
- Vedic study begins
- Family gatherings and feast

RAKSHA BANDHAN / RAKHI PURNIMA SIGNIFICANCE:
Festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters. Sisters tie a sacred thread (rakhi) on their brothers' wrists, and brothers pledge protection and give gifts.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Sisters tie rakhi with prayers
- Brothers give gifts and promise protection
- Family gatherings
- Exchange of sweets
- Priests tie rakhi on devotees' wrists for protection

LUNAR ECLIPSE:
Partial/Total Lunar Eclipse visible from 9:33 PM to 1:51 AM

ECLIPSE OBSERVANCES:
- Bath before and after eclipse
- Chanting of mantras during eclipse
- No eating during eclipse period
- Special prayers and charity
- Temples perform special rituals

COMBINED SIGNIFICANCE:
This rare combination of Upakarma, Rakhi, and Lunar Eclipse makes this day extremely powerful for spiritual practices and renewal.
