JUNE 17TH-20TH, 2026 - ANNUAL BRAHMOTSAVAM

Dates: Wednesday-Saturday, June 17-20, 2026
Temple Celebration: GRANDEST TEMPLE FESTIVAL OF THE YEAR

SIGNIFICANCE:
Annual Brahmotsavam is the most important and elaborate festival of the temple. "Brahmotsavam" means "festival of Brahma" - considered so sacred that even Lord Brahma would want to attend. This multi-day celebration honors Lord Venkateswara with elaborate rituals, processions, and sevas.

JUNE 17TH - ANKURARPANAM:
Date: Wednesday, June 17, 2026
Tithi: Chaturthi/Panchami
Event: Ceremonial beginning of Brahmotsavam

SIGNIFICANCE OF ANKURARPANAM:
Ceremonial planting of nine types of grains (navadhanya) in earthen pots filled with soil. The sprouting of seeds represents prosperity, divine blessings, and the growth of devotion. This ritual marks the auspicious beginning of the festival.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Planting ceremony with Vedic chants
- Kalasa sthapana (installation of sacred pots)
- Special prayers for successful completion of Brahmotsavam
- Temple decorations begin

JUNE 18TH-19TH - MAIN BRAHMOTSAVAM DAYS:
Dates: Thursday-Friday, June 18-19, 2026
Event: Peak celebration days with multiple sevas

DAILY SCHEDULE (Both Days):
- Early Morning: Suprabhatam and special abhishekams
- Morning: Snapana Thirumanjanam (sacred bath ceremony)
- Midday: Various Vahana Sevas (deity mounted on different vehicles)
- Evening: Grand processions

VAHANA SEVAS (Vehicle Processions):
- Garuda Vahanam (divine eagle mount)
- Hanumantha Vahanam (Lord Hanuman vehicle)
- Simha Vahanam (lion vehicle)
- Gaja Vahanam (elephant mount)
- Surya Prabha Vahanam (sun chariot)
- And other traditional vehicles

Each vahana has spiritual significance and represents different aspects of divine protection and blessings.

JUNE 20TH - CHAKRA SNANAM / DWAJAVAROHANAM:
Date: Saturday, June 20, 2026
Event: Grand conclusion of Brahmotsavam

CHAKRA SNANAM SIGNIFICANCE:
The final and most important day of Brahmotsavam. "Chakra Snanam" involves the ceremonial bathing of Sudarshana Chakra (Lord Vishnu's divine discus) in sacred waters. This ritual symbolizes the cleansing of sins and bestowing of divine blessings.

DWAJAVAROHANAM:
Ceremonial lowering of the temple flag (Dhwaja), marking the official conclusion of the festival.

CELEBRATIONS:
- Sacred bath of Sudarshana Chakra
- Final grand procession
- Lowering of temple flag with Vedic chants
- Distribution of blessed water (theertham)
- Grand prasadam distribution
- Cultural programs concluding

OVERALL BRAHMOTSAVAM FEATURES:
- Continuous special poojas for four days
- Elaborate deity decorations (alankaram)
- Traditional music and dance performances
- Special prasadam daily
- Community participation and gatherings
- Spiritual discourses

DEVOTEE PARTICIPATION:
Devotees can sponsor:
- Daily sevas and abhishekams
- Vahana sevas
- Annadanam (food offerings)
- Cultural programs
- Decorations and garlands
