JULY 17, 2026 - DAKSHINAYANAM BEGINS

Date: Friday, July 17, 2026
Tithi: Panchami
Also on this day: Sri Andal Abhishekam
Temple Celebration: Special prayers marking sun's southward journey

SIGNIFICANCE:
Dakshinayanam marks the beginning of the sun's southward journey (from Cancer to Capricorn). This six-month period lasts until Makara Sankranti (mid-January). While Uttarayanam (northward) is considered auspicious for worldly activities, Dakshinayanam is considered ideal for spiritual practices, meditation, and inner growth.

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Time for spiritual pursuits
- Increased devotional activities
- Period for introspection and self-study
- Preparation for spiritual transformation

OBSERVANCES:
Special prayers and poojas are performed to seek blessings for the coming six months.

JULY 24, 2026 - TOLI EKADASI / SHAYANI EKADASI

Date: Friday, July 24, 2026
Tithi: Dwadasi (Ekadasi before it)
Also Known As: Ashadi Ekadasi, Padma Ekadasi
Temple Celebration: Special Ekadasi observances

SIGNIFICANCE:
Toli Ekadasi ("Toli" means "first" in Telugu) is the first Ekadasi of Dakshinayanam and marks a highly significant event in the Hindu calendar. Also called Shayani Ekadasi, it commemorates Lord Vishnu going into Yoga Nidra (divine sleep) for four months.

CHATURMASYA BEGINS:
This Ekadasi marks the beginning of Chaturmasya - a four-month period of increased spiritual activity lasting until Kartika Ekadasi (November). During this period:
- Lord Vishnu rests on Adishesha (cosmic serpent)
- No auspicious ceremonies like weddings
- Increased fasting and spiritual practices
- Special dietary restrictions (varies by tradition)
- Daily prayers and bhajans

CELEBRATIONS:
- Fasting on Ekadasi day
- Special Vishnu abhishekam
- Reading of Vishnu Sahasranama
- Tulasi (basil plant) worship
- All-night vigil in some temples

OBSERVANCES:
Devotees observe complete or partial fast. Some avoid rice, grains, or specific vegetables during the four-month period.

JULY 28, 2026 - GURU PURNIMA

Date: Tuesday, July 28, 2026
Tithi: Prathama (after Purnima)
Also Known As: Vyasa Purnima
Also on this day: Sri Satyanarayana Swamy Pooja & Vratham
Temple Celebration: Special prayers honoring gurus

SIGNIFICANCE:
Guru Purnima is dedicated to expressing gratitude to all spiritual and academic teachers (Gurus). "Guru" means one who removes darkness (ignorance) and brings light (knowledge). This festival honors Sage Veda Vyasa, compiler of the Vedas and author of Mahabharata.

SAGE VYASA:
- Compiled the four Vedas
- Wrote the Mahabharata
- Composed 18 Puranas
- Systematized Vedic knowledge
- Considered the Adi Guru (original teacher)

CELEBRATIONS:
- Guru pooja (worship of the teacher)
- Offering prayers to Vyasa
- Students honor their teachers
- Spiritual discourses
- Pada puja (washing guru's feet - symbolically)
- Gurudakshina (offering to the teacher)
- Reading of sacred texts

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Acknowledging the role of teachers in life
- Expressing gratitude for knowledge received
- Renewing commitment to learning
- Seeking blessings for spiritual progress

MODERN OBSERVANCE:
Students thank their academic teachers, disciples honor their spiritual gurus, and everyone pays respect to those who have guided them in life.
