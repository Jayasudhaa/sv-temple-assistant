WHAT IS VASTRA SAMARPANAM?

Vastra Samarpana is a sacred devotional offering of clothes to the deity during rituals. This practice symbolizes deep respect and devotion towards God/Goddess, embodying the devotee's surrender and desire to clothe the divine with love and reverence.

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Expression of devotion and surrender to the divine
- Symbolic act of clothing God with love
- Offering of one's best to the deity
- Seeking blessings for prosperity and spiritual growth
- Traditional seva performed for centuries in Vaishnava temples

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS:

Q: Can I sponsor Vastra Samarpanam for a special occasion?
A: Yes! Many devotees sponsor for birthdays, anniversaries, or in memory of loved ones.

Q: What happens to the vastram after offering?
A: Sarees can be returned to you the next month if requested, or temple will use for deity.

Q: Can I attend the abhishekam ceremony?
A: Absolutely! Sponsors and their families are encouraged to attend.

Q: How far in advance should I book?
A: At least 1-2 weeks. Some months book up quickly, so earlier is better.

Q: What if I can't provide silk? Can I donate synthetic?
A: Temple tradition requires pure silk for deity offerings. Please opt for temple-provided option.

Q: Is this tax-deductible?
A: Yes, Sri Venkateswara Temple is a registered 501(c)(3) organization.

Q: Can I sponsor multiple months?
A: Yes, you can pre-book for multiple months. Contact temple for arrangements.
